ABOUT

Eilidh is an award-winning theatre director and dramaturg of Scottish descent with experience across musicals and new writing who currently works between London and her hometown of Birmingham.

Eilidh enjoys work that feeds off current political discourse to inspire innovation and deliver theatrical art back into its accessible and community-led origins. She is passionate about new writing, championing regional artists, and using theatre as an instrument for social change.

Eilidh has directed and assisted on productions in the West End, across the UK, and on international transfers including at the London Palladium, Tokyu Theatre Orb, Omnibus, and Blue Orange Theatre. Recently Associate Director on Dirty Rotten Scoundrels (Palladium / Tokyu Theatre Orb), and Director of The Things We Forget To Change which was awarded 2025 Best New Piece by Birmingham Fest.

As a dramaturg Eilidh has extensive experience collaborating with emerging writers on productions and is a Co-Founder of Camino Meets, a free monthly script development programme that she co-leads with the award-winning playwright Chris Fung. She is passionate about championing writers by improving access to opportunity and aims to nurture their skills through regular script reads, dramaturgy, and networking opportunities to build a creative team.
